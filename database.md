The Med-CORDEX centralized database allowing free data access has been hosted and managed by ENEA (big thanks to Lele Lombardi) from 2010 to 2025. Unfortunatly, it is not accessible anymore.  Currently, there is no centralized and open access to the whole Med-CORDEX data. 

You have various solutions to access the data:
  - A selection (6 Atmosphere RCMs, no coupled model) of the evaluation, historical and RCP scenario simulations is available for a limited number of variables and for various frequencies on the [C3S Climate Data Store](https://cds.climate.copernicus.eu/datasets/projections-cordex-domains-single-levels?tab=overview)  
  - You can also contact individually the data producing teams to access the data. 
Contact of the data producing team can be found on the [home page](https://med-cordex.github.io/)

More information will come when a better solution will become available. In particular, we plan to publish the [Med-CORDEX-CMIP6 Phase 3 baseline runs](cmip6-baseline_runs.md) on the ESGF-NG, starting in 2026.
